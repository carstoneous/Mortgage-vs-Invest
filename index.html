Let's compare two homeowners.

Both have a CHF 500,000 mortgage at 1.5% interest with a 30-year term. Each makes the required monthly mortgage payment. They also each have an additional CHF 2,000/month in spare cash.

Understanding the Payments

  • Regular mortgage payment: Both homeowners make the standard monthly payment required for a 30-year mortgage.
  • Extra CHF 2,000/month: This is additional money that can either go toward the mortgage or investments.
  • Over 15 years: The extra payments (CHF 2,000 × 12 × 15 = CHF 360,000) don't cover the entire mortgage, but they significantly accelerate the payoff due to reduced interest.

Path A: Paying off the mortgage

Person A makes extra payments toward their mortgage. This approach significantly reduces the time needed to pay off the loan. After approximately 12 years, it's fully paid off!

How Extra Payments Help

When you make extra payments on your mortgage:

  • The extra amount goes directly to reducing the principal
  • Less principal means less interest accrues each month
  • Each subsequent payment becomes more effective at reducing the principal
  • This creates an accelerating effect that pays off the mortgage much faster than 30 years

Path B: Investing instead

They stick to regular mortgage payments and invest the CHF 2,000/month. Let's assume a 6% annual return (stock market average).

The Math Behind It

When your mortgage interest rate is lower than your investment return, your money grows faster outside your house.

Investment Growth: CHF 2,000 × [(1 + 0.06/12)12×15 - 1] ÷ (0.06/12) ≈ CHF 600,000

Mortgage Interest Saved: CHF 2,000 × 1.5% × 15 years ≈ CHF 50,000

What you should consider

Investment returns vary

Past performance doesn't guarantee future results. Markets can be volatile in the short term.

Risk vs. guaranteed return

Paying off your mortgage offers a guaranteed return, while investing comes with varying levels of risk.

Liquidity & peace of mind

Investments offer better accessibility than home equity. However, being debt-free provides significant psychological benefits.
